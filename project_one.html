Analysis of global trends in coffee trade¶

Introduction¶

Coffee is a major part of many people's daily life. In fact, it is the third most popular drink in the world, beside water and tea, with over one billion people drinking coffee daily. Despite it's popularity, coffee can only be grown in within a region called the 'Coffee Belt' — a region roughly between 25° North and 30° South of the equator. As a result, the coffee market relies on healthy international trade. With such high demand, signficant strain is put on the countries and workers who produce coffee, raising concerns about the ethicality of the coffee market. The goal of this project is to better understand patterns in the global trade of coffee. I will be exploring a Kaggle dataset that contains data on several facets of coffee trade, including exportation, importation, and production of coffee. I would like to answer the questions of:

-Which countries are the largest exporters, importers, and consumers of coffee?

-Do imbalances within the global coffee trade exist?

-How has the coffee market changed over time?

As we can see, the amount of coffee imported is much higher than the amount of coffee exported.
